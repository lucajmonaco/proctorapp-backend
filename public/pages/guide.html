How to use InterviewMyCandidate

Everything from setting up a role to sending a candidate to your client. Roughly ten minutes end to end the first time.

1. Set up your company 2. Set up a role 3. Run a live interview 4. Send a one-way interview 5. Review the interview 6. Submit to your client 7. Good to know
Step 1

Set up your company

Your account belongs to a company. Everyone you invite shares the same job openings, recordings and reviews.

  1. Open Company from the menu.
  2. Share your company code with colleagues. They choose Join your company when they sign up.
  3. Admins can see the Reviews tab and delete reviews. Everyone else sees their own recordings.
Step 2

Set up a role

A job opening keeps every candidate for the same role together. A question template keeps every candidate answering the same questions.

  1. In Recordings Library, create a job opening for the role you are filling.
  2. In Async Interviews, build a question template: the question, seconds to think, seconds to answer.
  3. Save it once and reuse it for every candidate screening that role.
Thirty seconds to think and ninety seconds to answer suits most screening questions. Give more time for technical questions, less for warm-ups.
Step 3

Run a live interview

A live interview is you and the candidate on a call, recorded, with your questions on screen.

  1. Open Live Interviews and choose New Session.
  2. Add the candidate name, the role and your questions.
  3. Use Copy Invite to send the candidate their link and code. Send it from your own email so replies come to you.
  4. Join at the scheduled time. The candidate verifies their identity before joining.
  5. When you are done, press End Session. This saves the recording and asks you to rate the interview.
Always finish with End Session. Closing the tab leaves the recording unsaved.
Step 4

Send a one-way interview

The candidate records answers on their own time. Best for first-round screening when you do not want to spend an hour on calls.

  1. Open Async Interviews.
  2. Pick a template, add the candidate name and role, and set how many days the link stays open.
  3. Press Create link, then Copy invite message and send it from your own email.
  4. Watch the status change from pending to started to completed. Progress shows how many questions they have answered.

What the candidate sees: your company name, what is recorded and why, then a photo and photo ID check. Questions appear one at a time and only once recording has started, so nothing can be looked up in advance. Answers cannot be re-recorded.

Step 5

Review the interview

Finished interviews of both kinds land in Recordings Library, and in Sessions alongside everything else you have run.

  1. Transcript gives you the full conversation with speaker labels. Click any line to jump the video to that moment. Use Swap speaker labels if the two sides are the wrong way round.
  2. AI Summary gives a recommendation, strengths, concerns, skills and key quotes, plus a response integrity note.
  3. Add your own notes and a star rating, and upload the candidate resume.
The AI summary is a starting point, not a verdict. Every quote carries a timestamp so you can check it yourself before relying on it. Integrity signals are prompts to review the recording, never proof of cheating.
Step 6

Submit to your client

This is the part your client sees, so it carries your agency name rather than ours.

  1. On the recording, press Submit to Client and add the client contact and your recommendation.
  2. Use Preview Submission Page to see exactly what they will see.
  3. Copy email gives you a written email with the link already in it. Send it from your own inbox.
  4. Summary: shown to client toggles whether the interview summary appears on their page. Your concerns and integrity notes are never shown to a client.
Step 7

Good to know

Browsers. Chrome and Edge are safest for recording. Safari can be unreliable, and phones cannot share a screen.

Retention. Recordings, transcripts and identity photos are kept for about ninety days and then deleted. Download anything you need to keep.

Candidate questions. Candidates should contact you, not us. You own the relationship and can reissue a link at any time.

Still stuck. Email support@interviewmycandidate.com with the candidate name and roughly when the interview ran.
